# Lifestyle Changes — Meta Ad Hooks (men 40+)

*Voice: `ERIC_VOICE.md` (Eric speaking as himself — hedge-then-land, no shame,
no hype, absence not intent). Funnel: ad → `/lifestyle-changes` opt-in → guide
PDF → nurture drip → 14-day trial.*

All three point to `go.summithealth.app/lifestyle-changes`, each with its own
`utm_content` so winners are distinguishable. Measure **cost per
`freebie_lead_captured`** by `utm_content`; kill the losers, scale the winner.

Meta truncates primary text after ~125 characters — the **first line is the
hook**, so it's front-loaded before the "see more" cut.

---

## Hook 1 — The Problem

**Format:** Static image. *Visual:* a sticky note or discharge printout that just
says "lifestyle changes." Plain and real, not stock-y.
**CTA:** Download · **URL:** `…/lifestyle-changes?utm_campaign=mens-health-40&utm_content=problem`

**Primary text:**
> Your doctor said your cholesterol's creeping up. Make some lifestyle changes. And that was the whole plan.
>
> Here's the thing. You don't need an overhaul. Overhauls don't last — you go all-in for two weeks, then life happens.
>
> What works is smaller than you'd think. One habit. So small it's almost embarrassing. Run it a week, let it stick, then add the next.
>
> I wrote the whole method down. Two pages, made for men 40+. No app, no overhaul — just the first step and how to keep it.

**Headline:** `The "lifestyle changes" guide you didn't get`
**Description:** `For men 40+. One small thing at a time.`

## Hook 2 — The Method

**Format:** Static image. *Visual:* one checkbox vs. a long crossed-out list, or
"PICK ONE." big on screen.
**CTA:** Download · **URL:** `…/lifestyle-changes?utm_campaign=mens-health-40&utm_content=method`

**Primary text:**
> You've done the big overhaul before. New Year, new plan, all in. By March it's gone.
>
> That's not you failing. Those aren't really plans — they're just do-everything-at-once. Nothing's holding them up.
>
> Here's what holds: one thing at a time. A ten-minute walk after dinner, pinned to something you already do. Run it a week. Then add the next.
>
> Is it boring? A little. Boring is the part that works. Two pages, the whole method, made for men 40+.

**Headline:** `Stop trying to do everything at once`
**Description:** `The lifestyle change that survives a busy week.`

## Hook 3 — The Founder *(this one's the video)*

**Format:** Direct-to-camera video, 60–90s. Phone-shot, plain — over-produced
reads as "being sold to." This clip does triple duty: ad, day-10 drip email,
landing page.
**CTA:** Download · **URL:** `…/lifestyle-changes?utm_campaign=mens-health-40&utm_content=founder`

**Primary text:**
> My doctor told me to make some lifestyle changes. That was it. No plan, no how.
>
> Same thing happened to a lot of guys I know. So I built the thing I went looking for and couldn't find — a coach that doesn't bark orders, runs on one small habit at a time, and sticks around on the days that get away from you. Too slammed, slipped your mind, no time.
>
> I put the first steps in a free guide for men 40+. Ninety seconds on why, then it's yours.

**Headline:** `Why I built this`
**Description:** `A coach for men 40+ told to "make changes."`

**Video script (beat sheet):**
1. **First 3 seconds (straight to camera):** *"If your doctor told you to make some lifestyle changes — and that was pretty much the whole conversation — this is for you."*
2. **Who:** *"I'm Eric. Same thing happened to me."*
3. **Why the usual answer falls apart:** *"Every all-in plan I tried was gone by week three. Not because I'm lazy. Because there was no actual plan — just do everything at once."*
4. **The shift:** *"What works is smaller. One habit at a time. So small it's almost embarrassing. Run it till it sticks, then add the next."*
5. **Why I built it:** *"So I made the coach I went looking for and couldn't find. Direct, no shame, still there on the days that get away from you."*
6. **CTA:** *"First steps are in a free guide. Link's right here. Go grab it — then go do the one small thing today."*

*Dry humor is welcome in beats 3–4; drop it if it feels forced. Also run a still
of your face + the headline as a static ad — cheap way to tell whether it's the
message or the video doing the work.*
