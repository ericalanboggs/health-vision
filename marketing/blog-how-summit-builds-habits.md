# How Summit Actually Helps You Build Habits (And Why Most Apps Don't)

*By Coach Eric Boggs, Founder of Summit Health*

---

A friend of mine recently signed up for Summit. Two days in, he texted me: *"What's your POV on how to form a habit? What's the secret sauce?"*

It's a fair question. There's no shortage of habit apps. Most of them boil down to the same thing: set a goal, check a box, feel bad when you miss a day. That model works for about a week.

Summit is different — not because we cracked some magic formula, but because we designed around how behavior change actually works in real life. Here's the philosophy behind everything we do.

---

## 1. Start with what fits your life right now

Most apps hand you a list of ideal habits and say "go." Summit does the opposite — it right-sizes habits to your current reality. When you set up, our AI considers how much time you actually have, what you're drawn to, and what's realistic for your schedule. The goal isn't to build the perfect routine on day one. It's to start with something you'll actually do.

## 2. Connect every habit to your "why"

A habit without a reason behind it is just a task on a list. During onboarding, Summit asks you to define your health vision — what you're working toward and why it matters. That vision isn't a one-time exercise. It shows up in your reminders, your coaching, and your weekly digest. On the days when motivation dips, your "why" is what keeps the habit alive.

## 3. Prompt you at the right moments

Timing is everything. A reminder at 6 AM doesn't help if you planned to walk after work. Summit sends reminders and follow-ups based on when you said you'd do things — and checks in at your preferred time to ask how it went. It's the difference between a helpful nudge and noise you learn to ignore.

## 4. Build self-awareness through reflection

Every week, Summit prompts you to reflect: what worked, what didn't, and what you want to adjust. This isn't homework — it's how you start noticing patterns. Maybe you always skip your evening habit on Wednesdays. Maybe your best weeks start with a morning walk. Reflection turns unconscious behavior into conscious understanding, and that's where real growth happens.

## 5. Make adjusting easier than quitting

Here's the thing most apps get wrong: when a habit stops working, the only option is to delete it or feel guilty. Summit has a built-in pivot system. Text "BACKUP" and Summit AI will suggest a more manageable version of any habit — fewer days, shorter duration, a simpler version. Adjusting is always better than abandoning, and we make it easy to downshift in the moment.

## 6. Coach with the right tone

The voice behind Summit matters. We're not a drill sergeant. We're not a cheerleader. We're somewhere in between — encouraging without being patronizing, direct without being cold. Think of it as a friend who actually follows up. Someone who notices when you're struggling and offers help instead of a streak counter.

## 7. Fill the knowledge gaps

Sometimes you know *what* habit you want to build but not *how* to do it well. Your Weekly Digest delivers personalized resources — articles, videos, and practical tips — matched to your specific habits and where you are in your journey. It's not generic wellness content. It's curated for you, and it all lives in your Guides library for when you're ready.

## 8. Real humans when it matters

AI handles the daily rhythm — reminders, tracking, coaching responses, digests. But some moments need a real person. Summit offers 1-on-1 coaching sessions where you can talk through what's working, what's blocking you, and what to try next. It's the combination of consistent AI support and occasional human depth that makes the system work.

---

## The bottom line

Habits stick when they're **small enough to start**, **meaningful enough to care about**, and **supported enough to survive real life**.

That's not a hack. It's not a streak. It's a system designed around how people actually change — with flexibility, self-awareness, and support at every step.
