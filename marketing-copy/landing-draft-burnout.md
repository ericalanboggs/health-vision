## What This Is Really About

### Recovery is the start. Alignment is the point.

Most burnout advice treats you like a machine that needs better maintenance — sleep more, breathe more, optimize your recovery so you can keep producing at the same pace. That's not this.

The daily habits are real, and they help. But the quieter work underneath is coming back to what actually matters to you, and getting protective of the time and energy it takes. Sometimes that surfaces uncomfortable truths: a boundary you've never set, a yes you keep giving that should be a no, a conflict you've been avoiding because facing it is hard. Righting the ship usually means addressing those — not out-disciplining them.

This isn't coaching to make you better at your current job. It's coaching to keep you healthfully aligned to your own vision, mission, and values — whatever those turn out to be. As you come back to what matters, your sense of what you want may shift. That's allowed, even encouraged. Summit's job is to give you room to explore it in the small, real increments of time you actually have — not hand you another grand plan you'll abandon by Thursday.
