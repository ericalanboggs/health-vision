## The Philosophy

### What I believe about health change

**1. Vision before habits.**
Most apps ask "what habit do you want to build?" That's the wrong question. The right question is: "What does health mean to *you*?" When you're clear on your vision, the right habits become obvious.

**2. Reflection beats tracking.**
Logging every meal and step doesn't teach you anything. Pausing weekly to ask "what worked?" does. The habit that matters most is thinking‚Äînot tracking.

**3. Small experiments, not big commitments.**
"I'm going to work out 5 days a week" is a setup for failure. "I'm going to try a 10-minute walk after lunch and see how it feels" is how real change happens.

**4. Support should fit your life.**
Most people don't need a full-time health coach. They need a nudge at the right moment. AI can handle the daily reminders. Humans should step in when it actually matters.

**5. SMS is underrated.**
We're all drowning in apps. But texts? We read those. A simple text reminder at the right time is worth more than a fancy dashboard you never open.

## The Approach

### How Summit actually works

**Your vision is the foundation.**
Before any habits, you'll clarify what health means to you‚Äînot a generic goal, but your personal North Star. This becomes the anchor for everything.

**Everything cascades from your vision.**
The habits AI suggests? Based on your vision. The content we curate? Matched to what you're working on. The coaching support? Focused on your goals.

**Weekly reflection is the engine.**
Each week, you'll answer three simple questions: What worked? What didn't? What will you try next? This is where the real learning happens.

**SMS keeps it simple.**
Reminders, check-ins, and support‚Äîall via text. No app to open. No dashboard to maintain. Just messages that meet you where you are.

**Human support when it counts.**
AI handles the routine. A real coach steps in for the breakthroughs and the breakdowns‚Äîthe moments when you actually need a person.
